# Expressed Interest Signals V4: 300-Account Universe

Date: 2026-06-20

Universe: [data/health_systems_300.csv](../data/health_systems_300.csv)

The 300-account universe is a GTM coverage list, not a paid Definitive-style official rank. It is anchored on public largest-IDN references and expanded with large regional systems, academic systems, children’s systems, public systems, and specialty systems that matter for admin AI / RCM / patient access.

Inclusion rule: include accounts only where a health-system leader, future-dated RFP, testimony, conference/podcast, case study, or public post expresses interest in solving an operational problem. Vendor purchases alone do not qualify unless the source also states the health system's problem or intent. Archived RFPs are excluded from active leads.

## Source Families That Are Working

- Becker's CFO + Revenue Cycle Podcast: high-yield for RCM leaders expressing pain and AI interest.
- Becker's Healthcare Podcast / Digital Health + Health IT: high-yield for CIO/CDIO/innovation leaders.
- Public testimony: high-signal when a health system states AI/admin burden/access strategy.
- Future-dated RFPs: highest intent when the proposal deadline is still open and the use case matches patient access, call center, referral/fax, prior auth, billing, pharmacy operations, contract management, underpayments, or workflow automation.
- Health-system-authored case studies: useful when the system itself describes the problem, not just a vendor success story.

## Use-Case Source Buckets To Add To Daily Scans

Use customer-originated artifacts as the anchor evidence: future-dated health-system RFPs, health-system-authored operational writeups, public testimony, procurement Q&A, board packets, job posts, and operator interviews. Do not anchor a source card on a vendor blog, demo page, or customer-story page unless it links back to a health-system-side artifact. Archived RFPs are useful for query design and market vocabulary, but they should not appear as active buying motions.

- Faxes: search for AI fax, fax triage, RightFax, OnBase queue, faxed orders, and faxed referrals. Keep Stanford's FastFax writeup as customer-authored workflow evidence; add RFPs only when their due date is after the scan date.
- Referrals: search for referral management, faxed referral, order transcription, referral coordination, referral leakage, care transition, and closed-loop referral. Add RFPs only when their due date is after the scan date.
- Document management: search for document management, scanning, OCR, fax-line integration, document indexing, chart attachment, document audit history, and document activity reporting. Add RFPs only when their due date is after the scan date.
- Contract intelligence: search for payer contract management, contract maintenance, allowable calculation, reimbursement accuracy validation, contract interpretation, managed care contracts, and expected reimbursement. Add RFPs only when their due date is after the scan date.
- Underpayments: search for underpayment recovery, payment variance, actual payment less than anticipated, appeals, historical claims, and root-cause analysis. Add RFPs only when their due date is after the scan date.
- Pharmacy operations: search for pharmacy automation, central fill, specialty pharmacy, automated medication dispensing, pharmacy billing technology, and prescription fulfillment. Add RFPs only when their due date is after the scan date.

## Source Families To Deprioritize

- Generic "healthcare AI market" articles.
- Vendor customer lists without a health-system-side quote.
- Clinical-only AI, imaging, ambient scribe, diagnostics, research, drug discovery, and AI governance unless connected to admin burden, access, RCM, prior auth, claims/denials, referrals/fax, scheduling, or contact center.
